Warranties & Product Liability — Teaching Map

1. Express Warranties — statements, descriptions, samples
  • Creation: seller affirmations, descriptions, models; ads vs puffery
  • Breach: goods don’t conform to the affirmation/description/sample
  • Defenses: puffery (opinions), inconsistency, disclaimer limits
  • Example: Toaster box says “Nonstick crumb tray—cleans in 5 sec.” Tray has no coating and gums up. → Likely breach of express warranty.
  • Class hypo: “Lifetime” water bottle ad—what would a reasonable buyer understand? Draft the jury instruction.
2. Implied Warranty of Merchantability — fit for ordinary purpose
  • Merchant seller required; average quality; properly packaged/labeled
  • Breach: ordinary use fails due to defect
  • Example: New lawnmower stalls after 3 minutes under ordinary mowing load → merchantability issue.
  • Quick check: Would a typical buyer find the goods acceptable for ordinary use?
3. Implied Warranty of Fitness for a Particular Purpose
  • Seller knows buyer’s particular purpose; buyer relies on seller’s skill
  • Example: “Need boots for icy trails.” Clerk recommends hiking boots that lack ice traction → breach if they fail on ice.
  • Class exercise: Who bears burden to show reliance? Spot reliance language in a transcript.
4. Disclaimers & Limitations
  • “As is” / conspicuous; fitness disclaimers must be in writing and conspicuous
  • Limitations of remedy (e.g., repair/replace) subject to unconscionability & failure of essential purpose
  • Consumer layer: Magnuson–Moss may restrict disclaimers for written consumer warranties
  • Example: Used car sold “AS IS” with bold, all-caps window sticker—what remains claimable?
5. Privity & Standing
  • UCC alternatives A/B/C for third-party beneficiaries; remote purchasers
  • Economic loss rule interplay with tort claims
  • Example: Remote Amazon buyer sues manufacturer for warranty breach; does state adopt Alternative C?
6. Remedies & Damages
  • Benefit-of-the-bargain expectancy
  • Incidental & consequential damages (foreseeability; limitation clauses)
  • Notice requirement & statute of limitations (UCC default 4 years)
  • Example: Restaurant’s broken mixer causes lost profits—are they consequential and recoverable?
7. Tort Overlap (compare/contrast in class)
  • Strict products liability (manufacturing/design/warning defects)
  • Negligence; negligent misrepresentation; fraud
  • Where warranty remedies are better/worse than tort
8. Rapid-Fire Hypos (10–15 min)
  1. Express vs Puffery: “World’s best drill” vs “delivers 750 in-lbs torque.”
  2. Merchantability: Sealed baby formula with off-odor—what proofs?
  3. Fitness: Kayak recommended for “ocean fishing,” fails in mild surf.
  4. Disclaimer clash: “Full warranty” brochure + tiny “as is” invoice.
  5. Privity: Grandparent buys stroller; injury to grandchild—standing?
